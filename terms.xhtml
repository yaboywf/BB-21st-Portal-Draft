Cache Storage

What is cache storage?

  • Cache storage is primarily used to reduce latency and improve load times
  • They do so by storing copies of files (like images, scripts, and web pages) locally, the browser can retrieve them quickly without needing to fetch them from the server every time you visits us.

How we use cache storage

  • We would automatically cache static pages (pages that do not interact with the server) onto your device.
  • This will enable shorter loading time, as well as enable you to continue access to our website even when without internet access.
  • However, all admin pages (identifiable by the URL containing '/admin') will not be cached to prevent unauthorised access.

Controlling Caching

  • You may want to disable caching or clear caching
  • To disable caching, please vist our settings page.
  • To clear cache, please proceed to the following websites:
    • Google Chrome: https://support.google.com/accounts/answer/61416
    • Microsoft Edge: https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/edge/learning-center/how-to-manage-and-clear-your-cache-and-cookies
    • Firefox: https://support.mozilla.org/en-US/kb/how-clear-firefox-cache
    • Safari: https://support.apple.com/en-us/105082
    • Opera: https://blogs.opera.com/tips-and-tricks/2023/04/clean-browser-and-remove-trackers/

Cookies

What are cookies?

  • Cookies are small pieces of text sent to your browser by a website you visit
  • Help websites remember information about your visit, which can both make it easier to visit the site again and make the site more useful to you
  • Cookies come in several forms:
    • First-party cookies
    • Third-party cookies
    • Session cookies
    • Persistent cookies

How we use cookies

We utilize first-party cookies that are also persistent cookies. These cookies are designed specifically to remember user inputs, providing a more personalized and convenient experience as you navigate our platform. By storing information such as preferences or previous form entries, these cookies enable us to retain relevant data across sessions, which enhances usability and minimizes the need for repetitive input. This means that when you return to our site, your previous selections or information will still be available, making your experience smoother and more efficient. It’s important to note that these persistent cookies are used solely for the purpose of remembering user inputs and do not track or collect any personal information beyond what is necessary for this functionality.

We utilize session cookies to enhance the user experience on our platform. These cookies are essential for maintaining user authentication, ensuring that you remain logged in as you navigate through different pages. They are designed to provide seamless access to features that require authentication, and they play a crucial role in the functionality of our services. Without these session cookies, users would be required to log in repeatedly, which could hinder usability and create a frustrating experience. It’s important to note that these cookies are strictly necessary for the core operations of our website, and they do not track user activity beyond the duration of your session.

Our platform does not send third-party cookies directly to users. However, it is important to note that third-party cookies may still be generated and accessible when you interact with external services or websites integrated with our platform. These external services may use their own cookies to track user behavior, provide targeted advertisements, or enhance user experience. As a result, when using such services, users may encounter third-party cookies that are not initiated by our platform. We encourage users to review the privacy policies of these external services to understand how they handle cookies and user data.

Local Storage

What is local storage?

  • Local storage is a web storage feature that allows websites to store data directly in a user's web browser.
  • This data persists even after the browser is closed and reopened.
  • Local storage is stored as a key-value pair.

How we use local storage

  • We use local storage to store information like the status of guides as well as user settings.
  • Local storage can enhance user experience by saving preferences, so you do not need to adjust your prefered settings every time you visit our site.
  • We do not keep any personal data in the local storage key value pair.

Controlling local storage

  • You cannot disable to disable local storage, however you are able to clear it.
  • To clear local storage, please vist our settings page, OR
  • If you are familiar with browser developer tools, you can manually clear local storage.
